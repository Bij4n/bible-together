# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Open Bible is built around reading scripture together. Whether you contribute
code, translations, bug reports, or discussion, you're part of a community
that values the text and the people around it.

We're committed to a welcoming, harassment-free environment regardless of age,
ethnicity, religion (or lack of it), gender, sexual orientation, disability,
level of experience, or any other characteristic. Contributors from every
tradition and background are welcome.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful in issues, pull request reviews, and discussions.
- Assume good intent. If something reads as hostile, ask for clarification
  before responding in kind.
- Give and receive feedback graciously. Code review is about the code, not
  the person.
- Keep theological discussion focused on the product. Open Bible is software
  for reading scripture, not a forum for doctrinal debate.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks in any form.
- Publishing private information about someone without their consent.
- Sustained disruptive behaviour in issues, pull requests, or discussions.
- Using the project's channels to proselytize or to attack the faith
  (or lack of faith) of others.

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening a GitHub issue labelled "conduct".
Reports will be reviewed privately and handled proportionally — from a private
warning to a permanent ban from the project, depending on severity.
